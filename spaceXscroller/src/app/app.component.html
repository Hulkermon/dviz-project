The Space Race

of the 60s was a fierce competition between the US and USSR to see who could conquer space first, leading to a flurry of rocket launches and major breakthroughs in research as each country raced to outdo the other.

The Moonlanding

of 1969 put an unofficial end to the Space Race. With the first human footprints on a celestial body that wasn't the earth, the funding for space exploration stabilized and the research calmed down.

After the Space Race

there were fewer rocket launches because the focus shifted from a race for supremacy in space exploration to a more collaborative effort. Additionally, the cost of launching rockets and the technological challenges involved made it more difficult for countries and private companies to justify the expense.

The Founding of SpaceX

in 2002 would have a significant impact on the space industry. The goal of SpaceX is to reduce space transportation costs and eventually enable the colonization of Mars.

Successful Launch to Orbit

was achieved in 2008 and was a major milestone in space exploration history, as it marked the first time that a privately funded company had developed and launched a rocket into orbit.

So SpaceX was at the forefront of commercial spaceflight, but that's only the beginning.

Let's go back in time once more.

The Number of Governmental Space Agencies

had been growing steadily since the late 50s. These agencies were founded to conduct research and put communication as well as surveillance satellites into orbit.

Commercial Space Agencies

saw their first rise in popularity just before and during the Space Race, however these were heavily bound to existing governmental agencies and very different to todays commercial agencies.

By the Turn of the Millenia

commercial space agencies gained immense popularity due to an ever increasing demand for satellite launches, reduced barriers to entry in the market, changes in government policies, and public interest in space tourism.

SpaceX was founded alongside these first commercial agencies.

It was their next breakthrough which shook the world.

The First orbital Rocket to Land back on earth

changed the game forever. With cheap space travel coming into view on the horizon, many more commercial space agencies and even a few private agencies were founded globally. Some of them would pursue their own research to reduce costs of space travel.

Not only did SpaceX revolutionize space exploration technology, almost more importantly did they spark innovation in an industry that was growing dangerously stale.

Comparing Agencies to Launches

shows that the founding of commercial agencies may lead to more rockets launched, since profit oriented companies will always need to generate money. In contrary to government agencies, they do this by launching satellites or any other payloads into orbit.

A slow take-over by the Commercial Sector

had started after the space race. As time passed, technological advancements were made and by landing those booster rockets, costs were reduced massively. What awaits now is the turning point in history where commercial space exploration and travel will overtake government funded operations.

The recent founding of many new commercial space agencies, many of which having innovative ideas to reduce costs even further, may very likely lead to more rockets launched and more rapid research & development than the industry has ever seen before.

Worlds first successful

Booster Landing

SpaceX's successful landing of the first booster rocket was a significant achievement because it marked the first time that a rocket could be reused after an orbital launch. This reduced the cost of launching payloads into space and made it more affordable for organizations and individuals to send satellites and other payloads into orbit. Until this point, rockets were typically used for a single mission and then discarded, either by crashing into the ocean or by burning up in the Earth's atmosphere.
